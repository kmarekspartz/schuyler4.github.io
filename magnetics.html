Magnetics

So we know that charge has a polarity, like charges repel and unlike charges attract. It has not been mentioned yet, but this idea can be abstracted slightly with the concept of an electric field. Basically, if we know some space is not electrically neutral, that is there are some charges nearby. We can come up with a function that takes in a point in space, and a charge that may be placed at that point in space, and outputs the magnitude and direction of the force that this charge would experience. This is basically what an electric field is, a way to quantify possible electric interactions without dealing with individual charges. However, the electric force is not the only way that charges can interact with each other. Moving charges can also interact through the magnetic force. And, like there is an electric field with an electric force, there is a magnetic field for the magnetic force. The only difference, is that for a magnetic field, the charges speed and direction of motion must also be taken into account. As the magnetic force is an interaction between moving charges. The magnetic field is often represented visually by drawing magnetic field lines. Also, if this magnetic field summed within an area area, it is called magnetic flux (Ï†).
